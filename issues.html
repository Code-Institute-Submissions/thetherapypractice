Greif and Loss

Deat of a loved one can trigger overwhelming emotions, ranging from; shock; disbeleif; betrayal and sadness. Greif isnt only a response to death, it can be triggered by any type of loss, for example; moving home; divorce and changing career. Every loss is indivdual and valid. No two people will have the same reaction to the same event, our greiving mechanisms are a compilation of many factors, such as; our life experiences; our rearing and our personality.

Depression

Living with depression can make it difficult to cope with, daily life, for example; finding work; sustaining relationships and general inclusion in society. There has been a massive shift, but there is still a MAJOR societal stigma surrounding depression. The facts speak for themselves 2323211 people suffer with depression in London, UK alone! Discrimination experienced by those living with depression can make recovery even harder. It's nothing to be ashamed of and noone needs to suffer alone.

Anxiety

I have effective results with treating anxiety from a body orientated approach due to the varying physical symptoms experienced in the body. As soon as danger is recognised the brain sends a message to the pituitary gland. The pituitary gland in turn releases a hornmone which sends a message to the adrenal glands. The adrenal glands then release another hornmone CORTISOL which converts Glycogen stored in the liver into blood sugar to provide instant energy. This instant energy creates a variety of body processes which are carried out by the Autonomic Nervous System. This is a primitive response & the autonomic system can’t differentiate between the stress of being caught in traffic or being chased by a man-eating tiger. Avoiding anxiety & worrying about it makes it worse, one needs to recognise their triggers and begin to break their cycle of anxiety.

Issues surrounding Identity

Identity is part of a person’s self–conception & self perception. It can be related to; nationality,ethnicity,religion,social class , generation,locality or any kind of social group that has its own distinct culture. A persons identity is important because it aids the preservation of history and therefore provides individuals with a place where they feel they belong. As a second generation punjabi (Asian) woman i totally understand how it feels to struggle with some aspects of ones identity. My personal and proffesional experience of working with this has also allowed me to understand other forms of identity issues, such as; sexual orientation, gender and race. It is also ipmportant to understand that many of us have dual identity's; you may have a family-culture; a religious culture; a socio economic culture. All of these cultures form aspects of your identity and naturally we sometimes struggle to integrate our various identities.
